## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**No AI-slop tells.** The numbered Challenge markers (Challenge 1/2/3) are earned — each maps to a real, variable-count decision with genuine options-considered tradeoffs, not filler. Eyebrows double as scrollspy anchors, not decoration.

**Deterministic scan** (exit 2, same 2 finding types across all 3 files): `single-font` (**false positive**, same as homepage — both families load via one Google Fonts link + CSS var indirection, confirmed live), `em-dash-overuse` (22/10/6 across the three pages — proportionate to a personal-narrative format, not flagged as AI cadence).

**Browser evidence**: no console errors on any of the three pages. All images have descriptive, specific alt text. Section nav is a real `<nav aria-label="Case study sections">` landmark with correct anchor hrefs on all three pages. One transient rendering edge case: `[data-reveal]` sections can be caught at near-zero opacity if jumped to via a hash/programmatic scroll that outruns the IntersectionObserver — low severity (normal scrolling gives it time to fire), and fully bypassed for `prefers-reduced-motion` users.

## Overall Impression

Structurally the strongest surface on the site — the Decision/Options-considered/Impact pattern in Challenges is a genuinely senior-designer signal most portfolios skip. The biggest opportunity was the Outcome band's color (now fixed, verified live on all three pages after a cache false-alarm was ruled out), plus a couple of density/consistency rough edges between the three case studies.

## What's Working

1. **Decision/Options-considered pattern in Challenges** shows the "what else I considered and why not," not just the final answer — rare and valuable in portfolio writing.
2. **Reflection sections avoid the humble-brag trap** — rail-booking's Reflection is a genuine admission of a blind spot, not a disguised strength.
3. **The SplitMedia alternation rule is actually implemented correctly** (Context=right, Exploration=left, Validation=right) — verified live and in source, a small craft detail most portfolios wouldn't bother getting right.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] Outcome band hue mismatch (terracotta vs. olive) — FIXED, VERIFIED ON ALL THREE PAGES.**
Rated P0 specifically for case studies (more severe than the homepage's equivalent) because the band is full-bleed, ~450-650px tall, and lands at the emotional climax of *every* case study a recruiter reads — three separate "second brand color" moments, not one. One evidence agent's browser session showed rail-booking still terracotta; root-caused to `.claude/launch.json`'s dev server (`python3 -m http.server`) not sending no-cache headers — a stale cache in that one session, not a real code issue. Re-verified directly with a cache-busted reload: rail-booking, passkeys, and brand-governance all render the same olive (`#7A876A`) Outcome band, confirmed via computed styles and screenshot.

**[P1] Density imbalance: rail-booking's Validation section runs 4 paragraphs vs. 1-2 on its siblings.**
Sits right before the Outcome payoff, on the strongest case study, and is its least-edited section. **Fix**: cut to 2 tight paragraphs; the stakeholder-pushback beat could move into Challenges where the "options considered" framing already fits.

**[P1] Subnav label inconsistency: "AI Exploration" (brand-governance) vs. "Exploration" (passkeys, rail-booking).**
Same template slot (`AiSection`), different label — reads as accidental drift rather than an intentional editorial choice, since nothing else signals "this one is different in kind." **Fix**: either rename all three to reflect their actual content, or add an explicit visual cue for why brand-governance's is AI-specific.

**[P2] Run-on data-dense sentence in rail-booking Challenge 1** ("Seat selection data includes car type, seat class, direction of travel, table vs. window, reservation status, and accessibility needs, all variable by operator.") — ironic given the case study's own thesis is about avoiding exactly this kind of overload. **Fix**: trim to 3 representative examples.

**[P2] Cross-page inconsistency: Challenge image framing.** Brand-governance and passkeys mat several Challenge screenshots in a dark inset frame; rail-booking's Challenge images have no frame at all. Possibly intentional (self-framed device mockups), but worth a deliberate confirm.

**[P3] Theme-toggle-triggered scroll position loss** — clicking the toggle mid-scroll can snap the viewport, losing the reader's place.

- Validation section sub-shape differs all three ways (findings-list only / quote only / quote+images) — the template supports all combinations gracefully, but a reader going through all three back-to-back will notice "Validation" means something structurally different each time.
- Nav label and image-framing inconsistencies as noted in Priority Issues above.
